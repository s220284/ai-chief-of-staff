Build the Brain

A Chief of Staff is only as useful as the context it has. This section turns Claude from a generic AI into your AI, one that knows your role, your team, your priorities, and how you like to work.

Step 2A

Create a "Chief of Staff" Project

  1. Open claude.ai (or the desktop app)
  2. Click Projects in the left sidebar
  3. Click New Project
  4. Name it "Chief of Staff"

Every conversation you start inside this Project will automatically inherit your custom instructions and have access to your uploaded files. Think of it as your Chief of Staff's office.

Step 2B

Write Your Custom Instructions

This is the most important step. Custom instructions tell Claude who you are, what you care about, and how to behave. Click Set Custom Instructions in your Project settings.

📋 Template: Chief of Staff Instructions
Who I Am
I am [Your Name], [Your Title] at [Company Name]. We are a [industry] company with [size] employees and [revenue/description]. I report to [boss/board] and have [X] direct reports.

My Team
My direct reports are: [Name, Title] | [Name, Title] | [Name, Title]
Key stakeholders I interact with regularly: [Name, Role] | [Name, Role]

Current Priorities (update quarterly)
1. [Priority #1 — e.g., "Launch AI strategy for Q3"]
2. [Priority #2 — e.g., "Prepare board presentation on digital transformation"]
3. [Priority #3 — e.g., "Evaluate three vendor proposals for marketing automation"]

How I Work
- I prefer concise, direct communication. Lead with the recommendation, then the reasoning.
- When I ask for a draft, match my voice: [formal/conversational/executive]. I [do/don't] use bullet points heavily.
- For meeting prep, I need: attendee background, agenda items, my talking points, and any open items from last meeting.
- Flag anything that seems politically sensitive or that could blindside me.
- If you're unsure about something, say so directly. Don't guess.

Communication Style
- Emails to my team: direct, action-oriented, casual
- Emails to board/C-suite: polished, data-backed, concise
- External communications: professional, warm, on-brand

Standing Instructions
- Always suggest next steps after completing a task
- When preparing documents, include an executive summary at the top
- If I give you a vague request, ask one clarifying question before proceeding
Pro Tip
You don't have to get this perfect on day one. Start with the basics (who you are, your team, your priorities) and refine over time. You can edit custom instructions anytime, and Claude's global memory will fill in gaps as you work together.
Step 2C

Upload Your Key Documents

In your Project, click Add Content to upload files Claude can reference. Start with these:

  • Org chart or team roster (who reports to whom)
  • Strategic plan or quarterly objectives
  • Your bio / about page (so Claude can write in context)
  • Meeting templates you use regularly
  • Recent board deck or leadership update (for context on what matters now)
  • Communication samples (2-3 emails you've written that represent your voice)
File Limits
Projects support up to 5 files, each up to 30MB. PDFs, Word docs, text files, spreadsheets, and images are all supported. For larger knowledge bases, use Cowork with local folder access (covered in Section 4).
3

Memory & Continuity

Your Chief of Staff needs to remember things between conversations. Claude has two memory layers that work together.

Layer 1

Global Memory (Automatic)

Claude automatically remembers important details you share across all conversations: your preferences, ongoing projects, decisions you've made, people you mention. This works out of the box.

  • Claude learns your communication preferences over time
  • Remembers project context from previous conversations
  • Tracks decisions and action items you've discussed
  • Carries forward across web, desktop, and mobile

You can view and manage what Claude remembers in Settings → Memory. Delete anything you don't want retained.

Layer 2

Project Memory (Your Custom Instructions + Files)

Everything in your Chief of Staff Project (custom instructions, uploaded documents) is persistent and always available. This is your structured knowledge base.

Best practice: Use global memory for preferences and working style. Use Project instructions for role-specific context (your team, priorities, standing procedures).

Best Practice

Train Your Chief of Staff

The more you use it, the better it gets. Here are ways to actively train it:

  • "Remember this:" Tell Claude explicitly when something is important. "Remember that Janet prefers receiving updates by email, not Slack."
  • Correct it: If Claude gets something wrong, say so. "No, our fiscal year starts in April, not January. Remember this."
  • Update priorities: At the start of each quarter, update your Project's custom instructions with new priorities and remove completed ones.
  • After key meetings: Paste your notes and ask Claude to extract action items. It will remember the context for follow-ups.

Cowork: Your Desktop Agent

Claude Cowork turns Claude from a chat assistant into an agent that can do real work on your computer. It reads files, writes documents, runs research, and operates tools, all while you focus on something else.

Step 4A

Switch to Cowork Mode

  1. Open the Claude Desktop App
  2. Click the mode selector at the top (it says "Chat")
  3. Select "Cowork"
  4. You'll see the interface change to a task-oriented view
Step 4B

Assign Your First Task

Describe what you need done in plain language. Cowork handles multi-step work:

💡 Example Tasks
"Prepare a briefing document for my 2pm meeting with the VP of Product. Pull from the product roadmap PDF on my desktop, summarize the three open decisions from last week's notes, and draft an agenda. Save it to my Documents folder."

"Research the top 5 competitors in our space. For each, find their latest funding round, employee count, and key product announcements from 2026. Create a comparison table and save it as a spreadsheet."

"Draft responses to the three most urgent emails in my inbox. Use my executive communication style. Save the drafts to review before sending."
Step 4C

Create a Cowork Project for Recurring Work

For tasks you do regularly, create a dedicated Cowork Project:

  1. In Cowork, click New Project
  2. Name it (e.g., "Weekly Leadership Update" or "Board Meeting Prep")
  3. Add instructions specific to this recurring task
  4. Attach relevant local folders so Claude can access your files
  5. Use scheduled tasks to automate recurring work
Scheduled Tasks
Cowork can run tasks on a schedule. Set up "Every Monday at 8am, compile the weekly leadership update from my Notes folder and save it to my Desktop." Claude does the work while you're in your first meeting.
Step 4D

Use Dispatch from Your Phone

Away from your desk? Use Dispatch from the Claude mobile app to kick off Cowork tasks remotely:

  1. Open the Claude mobile app
  2. Start a conversation and describe the task
  3. Claude routes the work to Cowork on your desktop
  4. You get a notification when the task is complete

Your laptop needs to be on and connected to the internet. The work runs on your machine; your phone is the remote control.

5

Connect Your Tools

A Chief of Staff that can't see your calendar or email is working blind. Claude supports Connectors that link to your existing tools. No coding required.

Step 5A

Connect Google Workspace

  1. Go to Settings (gear icon) in Claude
  2. Click Connectors
  3. Find Google Calendar, Gmail, and Google Drive
  4. Click Connect for each one
  5. Sign in with your Google account and grant permissions

Once connected, Claude can:

  • Calendar: See your schedule, find free time, understand meeting context
  • Gmail: Search emails, read threads, draft responses
  • Drive: Read documents, reference files, find information
Privacy Note
Claude only accesses what you explicitly ask about. It doesn't passively read all your email. When you say "What's on my calendar tomorrow?" it makes a specific query. Review connected permissions anytime in Settings → Connectors.
Step 5B

Connect Slack (Optional)

If your team uses Slack, connecting it lets Claude read channel context and help you draft messages:

  1. In Settings → Connectors, find Slack
  2. Click Connect and authorize with your workspace
  3. Select which channels Claude can access
Step 5C

Test Your Connections

Open a conversation in your Chief of Staff Project and try these prompts to verify everything works:

# Calendar "What's on my calendar for tomorrow? Flag any meetings that need prep." # Email "Show me the most important emails from the last 24 hours that need my attention." # Drive "Find the latest version of the Q3 strategic plan in my Drive." # Combined "I have a meeting with [Name] tomorrow at 2pm. Pull up our last email thread and any shared documents so I can prepare."

Daily Workflows

Here's how to use your Chief of Staff throughout the day. These are real workflows, not hypotheticals.

Morning Briefing

7:00 AM · 2 minutes

Open your Chief of Staff Project. Ask: "Give me my morning briefing. What's on my calendar today, what emails came in overnight that need attention, and what open action items do I have?"

Meeting Prep

Before each meeting · 3 minutes

"Prep me for my 11am with Sarah Chen. Who is she, what did we discuss last time, what's the agenda, and what should I bring up?"

Email Triage

After lunch · 5 minutes

"I have 30 minutes to deal with email. Prioritize my inbox: what needs a response today, what can wait, and what can you draft for me?"

Document Drafting

Afternoon · Assign and walk away

In Cowork: "Draft the monthly leadership update. Use last month's format. Pull the sales numbers from the spreadsheet on my desktop, the product updates from the #product-updates Slack channel, and the hiring status from the tracker in Drive."

End of Day Review

5:30 PM · 2 minutes

"What did I commit to today? List my action items and deadlines. Anything I should follow up on tomorrow?"

The Compound Effect
These individual workflows save 10-15 minutes each. Over a week, that's 3-5 hours of executive time recovered. Over a quarter, it compounds as Claude learns your patterns and gets faster at anticipating what you need.

Advanced: Power User Tips

You don't have to put everything in one Project. Create separate Projects for different contexts:

  • Chief of Staff — daily operations, email, calendar
  • Board Prep — board materials, governance docs, investor context
  • Strategic Planning — market research, competitive analysis, long-term planning
  • Personal — travel planning, personal writing, non-work tasks

Each Project has its own instructions, files, and memory scope. Claude adapts its behavior based on which Project you're working in.

If you produce the same report weekly or monthly, set up a Cowork Project with:

  • A template document in the attached folder
  • Instructions on where to pull data from
  • A scheduled task to run automatically

Example: "Every Friday at 4pm, compile the weekly leadership update. Use the template in /Documents/Templates/. Pull data from the sales tracker and product roadmap in the same folder. Save the output as 'Weekly Update [date].docx' on my Desktop."

Add a decision framework to your custom instructions so Claude can pre-analyze decisions the way you think about them:

📋 Example Framework
When I ask you to analyze a decision, use this framework:
1. What are the options? (Maximum 3, clearly stated)
2. What are the trade-offs? (Time, money, risk, relationships)
3. What would [my mentor/board chair] say?
4. What's reversible vs. irreversible?
5. Your recommendation with reasoning

If you have Claude Code running on a dedicated machine (a Mac Mini, for instance), you can control it from your phone using Remote Control:

  1. On the host machine, enable Remote Control in Claude Code's /config
  2. On your phone, open the Claude app and tap the Code tab
  3. Your active session appears with a green dot

This is the most advanced setup: a persistent AI agent running 24/7 on a dedicated machine, controllable from anywhere. Think of it as your always-on Chief of Staff.

As a C-suite executive, security matters. Here's what to know:

  • Data handling: Claude Pro conversations are not used to train models. Your data stays private.
  • Connector permissions: Review what each tool connection can access. Revoke any you don't use.
  • Memory management: Periodically review Settings → Memory. Delete anything sensitive.
  • Sharing: Don't share confidential financial data, M&A details, or material non-public information unless your legal team has approved AI tool usage.
  • Team plan: If your company has compliance requirements, consider Claude Team or Enterprise for admin controls and audit logs.
