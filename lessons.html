MODULE 1

The AI Development Landscape

LLMs vs. World Models

  • Large Language Models (LLMs) calculate the next best token. They are sophisticated pattern-matching engines operating on statistical probability.
  • World Models allow AI to understand physical space with self-awareness. This is the frontier that separates current AI from true general intelligence.
  • Jan LeCun raised $1B for AMI (Advanced Machine Intelligence) based on world model theory. The math to solve this does not exist yet.
  • This requires a "new calculus" level breakthrough. Historical parallel: Newton invented calculus to solve planetary motion, then turned 26.

The Bitter Lesson (Rich Sutton, 2019)

Throwing enough compute and storage at problems beats human expertise every time. LLMs beat Google's English-major-designed translation systems. The implication: advanced reasoning engines might solve the world model problem before mathematicians do.

Current State of the Field

Anthropic's team works 10x faster with advanced Claude models that are not publicly available. We are in a period where cultural debt, not technical debt, is the main challenge. Organizations are limited by their willingness to adopt, not by the technology itself.

MODULE 2

Token Economics & Business Impact

  • Individual scale: $100-500/day in token costs is invisible to a single power user. At personal scale, the distinction between tasks, workflows, and agents is academic.
  • Enterprise math: 30,000 workers x $20/day = $15M/year. This is a material line item that demands CFO attention.
  • "Year of the Token P&L" — CFOs will start tracking AI costs as a dedicated line item, similar to how cloud compute became its own budget category.
  • Jevons Paradox applies: Cheaper inference does not reduce spending. It creates an insatiable appetite for more use cases. Every efficiency gain gets reinvested into new applications.
  • The economic difference between tasks, workflows, and agents matters at enterprise scale (cost predictability, budget planning) rather than personal scale.
MODULE 3

AI Task Classification Framework

Three strict definitions from the Anthropic model. Be your own "buzzword police."

Task (Single Tool Call)

  • Extract, summarize, find, search — one primitive, one action
  • Deterministic: you are in charge, you understand the output
  • Relatively cheap, but can be a trap if poorly scoped
  • Example: "Search the CE database for X"

Workflow (Multi-Tool Call, Mapped Sequence)

  • A to B to C to D with decision trees you personally mapped
  • You understand the flow — like a waterfall project schedule
  • Can incorporate machine learning graders (40 cents vs $40 for GPUs)
  • Can mix AI decisions with old-school ML, data science, heuristics
  • Example: Analyze 15,000 blog posts for 75-100 writing features using ML graders, then feed results to LLM

Agent (Multi-Tool Call, Full Agency)

  • "Bring me the broomstick of the Wicked Witch of the West — I don't care how"
  • Unpredictable token burn — may write 3,000 lines of Python when a simple tool call existed
  • Cannot find a tool? It will build one. You will not know it spent $8 doing so.
  • Requires monitoring dashboards and evaluation systems
  • Example: GSD (Get Shit Done) framework — ask questions, then go make things happen

"I want to be chief of staff to Carolyn Everson. Well, that means reading emails every morning. That's not an agent. That's a workflow."

"An agent would be: bring me the broomstick. I don't care how you do it."

MODULE 4

Evaluation Systems & Quality Control

Most Primitive Eval: The Like Button

Like Facebook's thumbs up/down. Better than nothing — it is signal. Any feedback mechanism is better than flying blind.

Better Eval: The Spreadsheet Format

  • Column 1: What was asked
  • Column 2: Results delivered
  • Column 3: Letter grade (from you, the subject matter expert)
  • Column 4: What could be done differently/better

This gets re-ingested into the system. This is how it improves.

Canon Safe / Brand Room (Peppa Pig Example)

  • Full character knowledge base: voice profile, relationships, legal issues, safety constraints
  • Multiple critics: canon fidelity, legal compliance, relationship accuracy, brand protection
  • Test suites, certifications, security red-teaming, A/B testing, model drift monitoring
  • Positive exemplars required — hundreds per character. Newer models (4.7) respond to positive reinforcement; older models (4.6) used negative reinforcement
  • The "pink elephant problem": telling a model "no em dashes" makes it think about em dashes

"What gets measured gets managed."

MODULE 5

Building Your Intelligence Platform

Single Source of Truth (SST)

Start with a Google Sheet. Shelly's started as a Sheet and remains a Sheet. This is your master reference — the system needs to know who people are, what relationships exist, and what matters to you.

Daily Brief System

4x daily updates (2am, 6am, 2pm, 6pm) covering: TL;DR, urgent needs, schedule, client intelligence, market intelligence, follow-up actions, key priorities. The system reads your calendar, emails, and knowledge base to produce a personalized intelligence briefing.

Email Draft System

AI drafts responses based on context and relationship history. Human reviews and approves. Nothing sends automatically. The human remains the decision-maker; the AI handles the cognitive labor of drafting.

Customer Data Platform (CDP)

52,000 people tracked, 28,000 flagged as important. Geo-mapped by airport code for meeting coordination. Engagement scoring, lifecycle tracking, signal detection for job changes and relationship shifts.

Agent Monitoring

Dashboard prevents runaway costs ($300-1,500/day personal burn is normal). Notifications fire for anomalies. You must be able to see what your agents are spending and why.

Research Agent

Analyzes readership behavior + news landscape to choose top 10 stories daily, then drafts content using the top-ranked story. Combines audience intelligence with editorial judgment.

"We don't need 10 years of shit — we need 20 good examples of everything."

MODULE 6

Data Safety & Legal Constraints

  • Liability shift: 72 MSAs with Fortune 500 companies changed terms. Liability went from 2x-5x to unlimited for data breaches.
  • Insurance unavailable: CNA (45-year relationship) could not write the policy. The risk profile is uninsurable.
  • Palmer Group's solution: Stopped coding for clients early 2025. Advisory only — no data access, no Slack channels, email only.
  • For the student: Must parse value based on contractual obligations. NDAs, board confidentiality agreements, and fiduciary duties all constrain what data can enter any AI system.
  • Compartmentalization is key: Personal life data vs. business/board data must be strictly separated. Different systems, different access controls, different risk profiles.
